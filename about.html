About Me

Hey, I'm Adrian.

Full-Stack Developer · Designer · Problem-Solver

I'm a web developer based in Angeles City, Philippines — currently pursuing a BS in Information Technology at Holy Angel University with a focus on Web Development. I love turning ideas into real, usable products that people actually enjoy using.

When I'm not coding, you'll find me organizing computing events, helping out at the school library, or experimenting with new UI patterns in Figma. I believe great software starts with empathy — understanding the people who will use it.

My Story

From Curiosity to Code

I didn't start out knowing I wanted to be a developer. What drew me in was the idea that you could build something from nothing — type a few lines of code and suddenly there's a working app in front of you. That moment of creation is what keeps me going.

My journey started with HTML and CSS, just building simple pages for fun. It quickly grew into JavaScript, then full-stack development. Today, I work with Vue.js, React, Node.js, and MongoDB — and I'm always picking up new tools and frameworks.

What matters most to me isn't just making things work — it's making them work well. Fast load times, intuitive navigation, clean code, and thoughtful design are things I genuinely care about in every project I take on.

Beyond the Code

Real-World Experience

Outside of personal projects, I've taken on roles that have shaped who I am as much as coding has. Serving as staff for the School of Computing taught me how to coordinate teams and manage operations under pressure.

Working as a student library assistant gave me a deeper appreciation for systems and order. And leading logistics for Code Geeks — our computing events — sharpened my ability to plan, communicate, and keep things running smoothly.

These experiences have given me soft skills that complement my technical abilities: leadership, clear communication, adaptability, and a genuine love for teamwork.

What Drives Me

My Philosophy

01

User-First Thinking

Every pixel, every interaction, every millisecond of load time matters. I build with the end user in mind — because software exists to serve people, not the other way around.

02

Clean, Maintainable Code

Code is read far more often than it's written. I strive for clarity, consistency, and simplicity — so the next developer (or future me) can pick up where I left off without guesswork.

03

Always Learning

Technology moves fast, and I move with it. Whether it's a new framework, a design pattern, or a soft skill — I'm always looking for what I can improve next.

04

Collaboration Over Competition

The best products are built by teams, not individuals. I value open communication, shared ownership, and the kind of feedback that makes everyone's work stronger.
